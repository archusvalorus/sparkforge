## Identity

- **Arena name:** The Star Anvil — the hidden heart of the forge. Not where sparks
  are *tested*; where they are *judged* (become a star, a weapon, or material to break down).
- **Tests:** **Inevitability** — "Can you endure when the arena stops asking and starts deciding?"
- **Player-facing test line:** *"Can you bear what you have become?"*
- **Arena voice line:** *"The forge weighs what you became."*
- **Scale:** substantially larger than Arena 4 (Arena 4 = 2× Arenas 1–3; **Arena 5 = 2× Arena 4**).
  Open, exposed, ceremonial — the footprint matters because the boss occupies an enormous share of it.
- **Palette:** floor = deep obsidian-black, cold indigo undertones, star-metal veining,
  white-gold pressure scars, circular hammer marks. Boundary = white-hot gold edge with
  intermittent violet-black gravitational flicker ("forged," not electrical/mirrored).
- **Motif:** concentric compression rings, broken orbital arcs, hammered starburst
  fractures, radial impact lines, crescent gravity scours.
- **Danger color:** star-white / hard gold-white, with selective **gravity-violet** for
  pull / collapse warnings. (Discipline: danger language stays clearer than atmosphere; grand, not busy.)
- **Ambient:** dust motes drift *inward*; star-sparks fall and extinguish; parallax
  shimmer "under pressure"; between waves the arena is quiet, almost reverent.
- **Mote setup:** the grander and more *final* the Star Anvil feels, the more disturbing
  (and funny) Mote's interruption becomes. It must read as a true ending.

## Enemy family (teaches inevitability: pull · committed motion · mass)

1. **Gravemote** (early teacher) — small unstable gravity ember; drifts, emits localized
   pull, low direct damage. Lesson: *"Your position can be stolen."* Visual: dark core,
   thin star-white rim, violet gravity ripple.
2. **Star Needle** (mid) — forged lance of committed trajectory; locks a firing line,
   telegraphs clearly, commits to a high-speed linear dash/shot; punishes late movement,
   not random. Lesson: *"Decision beats panic."* Visual: long luminous star-shard, gold-white point.
3. **Anvilborn** (late/elite) — heavy star-metal construct; advances with stubborn
   certainty, occupies space, short shockwave slams, may anchor briefly. Pairs with
   Gravemote pulls + Star Needle lanes. Lesson: *"The arena is running out of room, and it means it."*

## Boss — The Unmade Star (axis: Inevitability) · MONUMENT BOSS

The **first monument boss** (see memory `monument-boss-class`): occupies ~the upper third
of the (doubled) arena, partially anchored, a set-piece — not a scaled-up unit. Player
fights in the lower two-thirds; attacks descend / radiate / collapse into the playfield.
Reference: a modernized *Secret of Evermore* screen-wide boss with 2026 arena-shooter readability.

- **Silhouette:** colossal asymmetrical star-body fused with forge structures — central
  star-core, fractured crown/anvil upper shell, orbiting fragments, broken radial arms,
  cracked luminous seams. "A thing being hammered into existence and failing to hold shape."
- **Phase 1 — Accretion** (measuring): *Accretion Orbit* (rotating orbital threat paths),
  *Collapse Marks* (delayed targeted ground markers), *Starfall Sequence* (ordered falling impacts).
- **Phase 2 — Weight of the Center** (gravitational dominance): major pull event; movement
  becomes deliberate; Gravemote pull logic scales into boss grammar; support Star Needles descend.
- **Phase 3 — Unmaking** (failing violently): broken cadence, wider orbital danger, faster
  Collapse resolution, forge-fracture impacts across the lower playfield.
- **Enrage — Final Compression** (low HP): brighter cracks, darker atmosphere, more violent
  warping, modestly accelerated cadence. "Accepted its own destruction; trying to force yours first."
- **Tells (must be exceptionally clean given the scale):** orbit lanes visibly wind up;
  Collapse Marks use distinct ground telegraphs; pull announced by visual compression toward
  the core; starfall sequences readably; NO overlapping unparseable tells.

## Earned skin — Star-Crossed Spark (family: "Starforged")

Unlocks after: clear Arena 5 → witness Mote's entrance → be one-shot. (Half-triumph, half-consequence.)
Cosmetic-only. See memory `skin-system-canon`.
- Visual: white-hot star core; deep indigo/midnight outer flame; detailed flame silhouette;
  sharper anime-focused eyes; constellation cracks across the body; orbiting star fragments;
  faint **inward-bending** gravity trail; possible mismatched star eyes (one bright, one eclipsed);
  a small cracked-star mark near the face.
- Motion: cleaner/more deliberate trail; level-up releases constellation motes; idle drifting star specks.
- Purpose: first major cosmetic reward — communicates *skins are expression, not power.*

## Mote entrance — "Mote the Terror" (a myth intrusion, not a boss reveal)

After the Unmade Star dies: (1) arena goes quiet → (2) rewards scatter → (3) a beat of
triumph → (4) ambient particles reverse → (5) star dust bends toward a point that shouldn't
exist → (6) screen distorts with neon-white + violent purple interference → (7) Mote resolves
at the arena edge → (8) looks at the player → (9) crosses with impossible speed → (10) kills
through all protections.
- Visual: very bright neon-white form, purple distortion edges, too-crisp silhouette (feels
  "from another layer of the app"), tiny body / enormous authority.
- Presence: sound briefly ducks/warps; arrival feels like the game glitched **on purpose**;
  uncomfortable for a moment, then funny.
- **Canonical behavior:** bypasses shields, DEF, invulnerability, Last Light, death prevention,
  normal damage rules. He doesn't hit hard — he **ends the run by script.**
- Speech bubble: *"Bet that hurt, heehee!"*

## Bestiary flavor

- **Gravemote** — "A failed spark compressed into hunger. It does not chase. It simply convinces the world to come closer."
- **Star Needle** — "Precision forged into malice. Once it chooses a line, something is expected to die on it."
- **Anvilborn** — "The forge made a body out of weight and impatience. It advances like the answer was already decided."
- **The Unmade Star** — "A star caught between becoming and breaking. The forge keeps striking. It keeps failing. You arrived at the worst possible moment."

  base (instance #1), and Boss Mode must be arena-aware to host it. See `monument-boss-class` memory.
- **Reuse check:** Gravemote pull, Collapse Marks, and Accretion Orbit likely reuse existing
  primitives (priority-target, zone/telegraph, orbital patterns from prior bosses) — audit before authoring new.
