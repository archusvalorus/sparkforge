# Sparkforge v2.0 Phase C — Growth Tree: Build Kickoff

*Written Jul 23 2026, after a codebase survey against
`growth-tree-creative-handoff-v2.0.md`. That document is the CREATIVE source of
truth and is not relitigated here — this is the build plan for it.*

---

## What the survey changed

The creative handoff's build note claims "the v1.9 capstones are the Growth
engine in disguise." **Mostly true, with three corrections that shape the unit
order:**

1. **There is no card-pool gating of any kind.** `UpgradeManager.drawCards`
   filters only on `tier < maxTier` plus the capstone focus rules. Terra's
   "unlock the Growth pool" is therefore **a new subsystem, not a flag** — and
   it's the same subsystem the banked `card-offer-eligibility-layer` needs
   (never offer an amplifier before its enabler) and the same one arena
   tree-gating will need. Build it once, deliberately, and three problems close.
   **This is why it goes first.**

2. **There is no reusable zone primitive.** What exists is three bespoke
   implementations — `nullBloomZones` (a tuple array with its own update loop),
   Everglow's eruption, and Windchill's storm. None of them supports what
   cultivated ground needs: **persistent, position-fixed, with differential
   effects on player vs enemies, and modifiable after creation by Terra+.**
   That's a real primitive and it deserves its own unit.

3. **Priority targeting is duplicated per capstone.** `findIronMaidenTarget`
   (boss → miniboss → nearest) is the pattern Defensive Flowers and the Tree's
   animal launcher both want. Extract it once when the first structure needs it,
   rather than writing a fourth copy.

Genuinely cheap, as promised: the tag itself (4 files, 3 lookup tables), the
tier→event ladder (Everglow's shape exactly), and the arena-origin priority
projectile (Iron Maiden / Erasure).

Genuinely expensive, as warned: **Vine Wall's impassable terrain.**

## Unit sequence

Each unit is one commit at DoD-pass, with a Brandon device validation between.

### C1.1 — Growth tag + the eligibility layer
The foundation. No Growth *content* yet beyond Terra itself.
- Add `growth` to `UpgradeManager.Tag`; wire the three lookup tables (card
  colour `UpgradeCardNode:41`, emoji `:71`, synergy hint `UpgradeManager:333`)
  and the buff tracker.
- Build a **provides/requires eligibility layer** in `drawCards`: a card may
  declare what it `provides` and what it `requires`. Hard-gate on unmet
  requirements. Terra `provides: .growth`; every other Growth card
  `requires: .growth`.
- **Terra** ships as a real card with immediate value, not a bare key.
- **DoD:** Growth cards never appear before Terra; Terra appears in the normal
  pool; picking Terra opens the pool from the next level-up; buff tracker shows
  a Growth chip in the right colour; build + zero new warnings.

### C1.2 — Cultivated ground (the zone primitive)
- A reusable `CultivatedZone` — persistent, fixed-position, with **differential
  effects** (enemies slowed; player effects layered later by the Tree).
- Terra creates the first zone. Zones are **capped and pooled** from day one
  per the handoff's performance warning.
- Terra+ modifies **all active zones**, so the modifier path exists before any
  Terra+ card does.
- **DoD:** zone renders in the Growth palette (distinct from health green),
  slows enemies inside, survives a full run, no frame cost at the cap.

### C1.2.5 — Terra placement + garden expansion (added Jul 23)
The agency layer, forced by the C1.2 device pass. Path A.
- **Placement:** picking Terra enters a paused tap-to-plant beat with a ghost
  preview; tap inside the arena to commit. Granted Terra (RANDOM opener) still
  auto-plants at Spark.
- **Expansion:** each subsequent Growth-tagged pick grows every cultivated zone
  by `growthExpansionPerCard`, capped at `maxZoneRadiusFactor × arena radius`.
- **DoD:** Terra plants where you tap, not under Spark; the garden visibly grows
  on each Growth pick; the zone never exceeds the cap or swallows the arena;
  granted Terra still auto-plants; build + zero new warnings.

### C1.3 — Defensive Flowers (the structure primitive)
- A persistent, player-owned, count-capped `GrowthStructureNode` that acquires
  and fires at targets. Extract the shared **priority-target** helper here.
- **Flowers grow ON cultivated ground only** (Brandon, Jul 24) — never loose in
  the arena. Permanent-up-to-cap. No babysitting.
- **DoD:** flowers visibly aim and fire at ~25% player ATK, respect the cap,
  plant only within the garden, never outlive a run, read unmistakably as
  player-owned.

> **DESIGN LAW — the whole Growth arc is ROOTED to the Terra plant.** Everything
> Growth does happens *on cultivated ground* and grows as the ground grows:
> flowers, seeds, walls, the Tree capstone are all expressions of that one
> cultivated claim spreading. Terra is the root; the rest is what sprouts from
> it. If a proposed Growth effect doesn't tie back to the ground, it probably
> belongs to a different tree (Decay) or a synergy — check before building it
> into Growth. (This is also *why* Terra is a hard dependency.)

### C1.4 — Seed Spore Shot
- `embed → kill → reproduce`, as a 3-tier offensive ladder.
- Reuses the existing projectile pipeline; the seed is enemy state.
- **DoD:** seeded enemies burst on death, secondary projectiles deal reduced
  damage, tier 3 re-embeds at reduced effectiveness without infinite chaining.

### C1.5 — Vine Wall
**The one genuinely new subsystem, and the one I'd most like to de-risk.**
See the open decision below — the recommendation is to ship a zone-based
fallback first and only attempt true collision if it proves cheap.
- **DoD:** enemies are meaningfully redirected or stopped; bosses behave per the
  chosen rule; no pathing stalls, no enemies stuck on geometry.

### C1.6 — Tree (the Growth capstone)
- Five tiers: Sapling → Rootreach → Shelter → Wild Domain → The Forest Wakes.
- Reuses C1.2's zone (expanding, not replaced), C1.3's targeting, Everglow's
  tier→event ladder, and the `CapstoneTimerHUD` for T5's cadence.
- **T5 launches a visibly wholesome woodland animal.** This is a hard creative
  requirement, not a nice-to-have — a green blob does not land the joke.
- **DoD:** the Tree visibly matures per tier, the zone expands without the Tree
  being replaced, T5 fires on a legible countdown, and the animal reads as an
  animal at gameplay speed.

### C1.7 — Terra+ card, dual-tag card, synergies, balance
- One Terra+ utility card (recommend **Rich Soil** — it exercises the
  modify-all-zones path already built in C1.2).
- One dual-tag Growth card, chosen for implementation compatibility.
- Growth synergy tiers wired at 3/5/7 like every other tree.
- **DoD:** Growth reaches ≥7 tagged cards, synergies fire, and a Growth build is
  playable start to finish without dominating or being dead weight.

## Decided — do not relitigate

*Answered by Brandon, Jul 23 2026.*

**1. Vine Wall ships the zone-based FALLBACK.** Heavily slow / repel normal
enemies; bosses pass through. "If it works as intended, great. If not, we tune
and fix." Real collision is not in Phase C scope — revisit only if the fallback
demonstrably fails to carry the fantasy.

**2. Terra is ONE TIER.** Its job is to open the options that grant the
effects — deliberately *not* prescriptive, so the player chooses what kind of
ecosystem to build. A ladder would make opting in feel like a tax.

**2b. Terra is PLACED, and Growth is ONE GROWING GARDEN (Path A) — added Jul 23
after the C1.2 device pass.**
- **Placement is a requisite, not a polish item.** RNG owns *when* Terra
  appears, so placement is the one axis of agency the player can be given — and
  it turns Terra from "a thing that happened" into "I claimed *that* ground".
  A chosen Terra is placed by the player (tap-to-plant beat while the level-up
  screen holds the world frozen; ghost preview → tap to commit). A *granted*
  Terra (the RANDOM Boss Mode opener, which has no pick screen) auto-plants at
  Spark — you cannot ask a player to place a card they did not choose.
- **Expansion model = Path A: one concentric garden that grows.** Every
  subsequent Growth-tagged card expands the existing territory by a fixed % —
  reusing `modifyAllCultivatedZones(radiusScale:)`, built ahead of need in
  C1.2. Structure cards (flowers) additionally place their structure; the
  expansion is the universal Growth heartbeat, structures are the payloads.
- **The zone is CAPPED below the arena radius.** If the garden could swallow the
  whole floor, "leaving cultivated ground stays a real decision" (handoff §8)
  dies. Cap at a fraction of the arena.
- **Path B (land spreading across the arena, patches connecting) is DEFERRED to
  the Verdant biome — this is the seasonal grammar working as designed:** the
  tree seeds the fantasy, the biome fulfills it. Path A ships on the general
  multi-zone primitive so B remains a later evolution, not a rewrite. A B-lite
  (place-anywhere, distance-gated maturation) was offered and set aside with B.
  Do not build B or B-lite into Phase C.

**3. Growth COUNTS toward Variegated Rainbow.** Confirmed rule: **any
non-neutral card counts toward the total.** Breadth stays honest because
omission is coming — see below.

**Corollary banked for later (not Phase C):** as trees multiply, arenas will
OMIT elements. Arenas 6–10 add a run-start prompt to **select an element to
omit**; Arena 11+ makes omissions authored per biome (a Growth biome has no
Fire). Count still under consideration. **The C1.1 eligibility layer is the
mechanism for this** — arena tree-gating and element omission are the same
filter with a different source. Do not build a second gating system.
See the `element-omission-rules` memory.

**Also banked:** the Tree's T5 animal launcher architecture — a pool of 5–10
woodland animals resolved as `base timer + damage mod (~150% Spark) +
per-animal modifier`, with a rare pet-summon tier (mountain lion, ~10s, roams
and mauls). Build the launcher against that three-part model from the start at
C1.6; bring the roster to Brandon rather than inventing it. See the
`tree-capstone-animal-pool` memory.

## Risks

- **Performance.** Growth stacks persistent zones, structures, projectiles and
  particles. Explicit caps and pooling from C1.2 onward, not retrofitted.
- **Scope.** Seven units is the largest phase in v2.0. C1.1–C1.3 are the
  foundation; if the phase needs trimming, C1.5 (Vine Wall) is the honest cut —
  Growth's identity survives without it, and the handoff's MVP list would still
  read as complete with a Terra+ card in its place.
- **Legibility.** Growth must not read as health-green. Palette is specified in
  the handoff; hold to it.
