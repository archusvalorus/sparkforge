## Read this first — the NEW card-tier system (design everything tier-aware)

v1.9 ships **card leveling**: a card you already own can be re-offered to
**level it up**, gaining power tiers, instead of being one-and-done. This
changes how you design cards — please design Arena 5's set (and think about
capstones) with this in mind.

**The rules:**
- **Tier matrix — every card is 1, 2, 3, or 5-tier. NO 4-tier.** Judge each
  ability: how many tiers can it meaningfully carry while staying fun? Some
  abilities are one-and-done (1-tier); some want a quick pair (2); some a
  standard arc (3); a special few earn a full epic ladder to 5.
- **Tier 5 = a capstone** — a qualitative twist, not just bigger numbers.
- **ONLY one tier-5 capstone card per skill tree** (Fire/Shock/Bleed/Guard/
  Void/Chill). Bounds the chaos.
- **Orthogonal to synergies.** Tag breadth still drives synergy tiers (3/5/7);
  card tiers are a *separate* depth axis. Deepen one card vs. spread across a
  tree is the tension we want.

**Two example ladders (for feel):**
- *Multishot* (the canonical 5-tier): L1 +1 shot → L2 3 shots/more spread → L3
  4 → L4 5 → **L5 capstone**: every ~50 bullets fires a giant 5×-damage shot.
- *Attack Speed* (a proposed Shock 5-tier): L1 +5% → L2 +25% → L3 +50% → L4
  +75% & projectiles fly faster/farther → **L5 capstone**: +100% AND you become
  a living laser — a separate constant beam that melts the nearest enemy
  (reduced damage; the sustain, not the burst). Could require owning 1 Shock
  card to unlock.

## Ask 1 — Arena 5 identity
The fifth and final standard arena — a **larger open plane** (no geometry/zones
yet; that's v2.1+). The anchor question: each arena *tests* something —
**Crucible = survival, Quench = positioning, Coilworks = rhythm, Mirrorwound =
certainty**. What does the **capstone** arena test? Give us: fantasy, palette
(floor/boundary/motif/danger + discipline notes), motif geometry, ambient
behavior, and a **voice line**. Bonus: how it sets up the Mote's arrival.

## Ask 2 — Enemy family
A new family taught in layers (early teacher → mid → elite), per the
established curriculum. Distinct pressure from arenas 1–4.

## Ask 3 — The fifth boss
A **new axis** — distinct from Titan (impact), Warden (pressure), Choir
(rhythm), Faceted Lie (deception). Silhouette, phases, tells, enrage, palette.
Completes the five-boss set for Boss Mode.

## Ask 4 — Card set (TIER-AWARE)
The Arena 5 card set. For **each** card give: tag(s) (dual-tag rule — counts
toward both trees), and its **tier ceiling (1/2/3/5)** with the effect at each
tier. Keep tag counts ≥7 everywhere. If one card is the natural **capstone**
for this arena's tree, design its L5. Avoid duplicating existing mechanics.

## Ask 5 — Earned Spark skin (art direction)
The reward that fires right after the Mote one-shots the player. How does the
**base Spark evolve**? (Baseline today: an ember with two small black eyes that
track movement.) Brandon's vision: more flame detail, the eyes morph to
**focused / anime-style**, plus particle effects. Give the visual language +
any motion notes. It's the first of a cosmetic-skin line, so a strong,
identity-setting look.

## Ask 6 — The Mote entrance ("Mote the Terror")
Creative direction for the scripted entrance moment (ties to
`docs/mote-v2.0-handoff.md` — VERY purple + neon-white at reveal). The intended
feeling: a level-5 surprise that **one-shots** existing players — "wait, wtf
just happened, what WAS that?" — dread and mystery, not a fair fight yet. How
does it look/announce itself? (The beatable Mote fight may be v2.1 — TBD.)
